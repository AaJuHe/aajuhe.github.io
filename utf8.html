Throughout the video, there were a number of terms I was familiar with, so finding something that was fairly foreign to me was a bit of a challenge. Mojibake was interesting to say the least; seeing how different characters were translated into something incomprehensible was pretty cool. Other than that, I chose to do some research on something that was not pretty new to me.

I stumbled across an article on the history of ASCII and its importance in terms of computers and technology in general. The thing that jumped out to me the most was how long it took for ASCII to be accepted as a standard text format. It apparently was finished in 1963 by former IBM employee Bob Bemer and ANSI; however, it was not widely accepted until 1981. I can only imagine how much fun it was to program and work on computers without a universal standard code. Something really nice about ASCII is that it is still used to this day despite being pretty much rendered obsolete by Unicode and UTF-8.
